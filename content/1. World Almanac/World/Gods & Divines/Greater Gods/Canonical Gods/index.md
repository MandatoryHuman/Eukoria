> [!quote] "The Canonical Gods are the frictionless gears of the cosmos. Where their power flows, the laws of physics and nature behave exactly as they should. Rely on them, for they are the bedrock of our sanity."

Canonical Gods are a classification of [[1. World Almanac/World/Gods & Divines/Greater Gods/index|Greater Gods]] whose existence is defined by natural harmony. In the cosmic geometry of Eukoria, a Canonical God is comprised of any two adjacent Essences on the cosmic wheel.
Because their essences flow into one another smoothly without contradiction, these deities form the bedrock of the orthodox universe. They govern the established laws of both nature and society, predictable magic, and the stable reality that mortals rely on to survive.

## The [[1. World Almanac/World/Gods & Divines/Greater Gods/Canonical Gods/Gods of Magic/index|Gods of Magic]]

A highly specific and universally revered sub-category of the Canonical Gods are the Gods of Magic. These deities are comprised of two adjacent _Fundamental Essences_ (excluding the Basic Essences of Presence and Absence).
Because they represent a perfect, stable loop of fundamental reality, each of these gods governs and represents one of the orthodox [[Traditions of Magic]] in Eukoria.

## Other Canonical Gods

While the Gods of Magic exist solely within the fundamental wheel, other Canonical Gods exist by combining a Fundamental Essence with an adjacent Basic Essence (Presence or Absence).
