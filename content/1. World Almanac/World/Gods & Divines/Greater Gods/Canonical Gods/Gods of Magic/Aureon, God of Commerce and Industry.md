Aureon is the Greater God of Commerce and Industry, and the divine arbiter of Arcane Magic. Representing the orthodox, calculated blend of the Fundamental Essences of Mind and Matter, Aureon teaches that physical reality can and should be manipulated through pure intellect. To his followers, magic is not a chaotic force to be tamed, but a precise equation written in the language of the cosmos, waiting to be solved to construct miraculous wonders of artifice.

![[Aureon.webp|400]] 
Unlike deities driven by emotion or vengeance, Aureon operates on strict logic and binding agreements. As one of the [[1. World Almanac/World/Gods & Divines/Greater Gods/Canonical Gods/index|Canonical]] [[1. World Almanac/World/Gods & Divines/Greater Gods/Canonical Gods/Gods of Magic/index]], he is pact-bound to remain completely neutral in his influence upon the world's magics. Because of this perfectly balanced, unmitigated nature, Aureon frequently takes on the role of an impartial arbiter in conflicts between other gods. In the mortal realm, this divine neutrality translates to an obsession with binding trade and infrastructure; his high priests are famous for drafting iron-clad contracts, frequently working in tandem with the state-sanctioned spies and diplomats of [[Lethos, God of Trickery]], to execute highly advantageous financial and political manoeuvres. His worship is heavily ingrained in the wealthy nation of [[1. World Almanac/World/Locations/Eukoria/States/Meridia/index]], where his tenets help the ruling Board of the Concordat maintain their ruthless mercantile oligarchy.

The followers of Aureon, most notably traditional Wizards and Magi, practice an art that is seen as upholding the natural laws of the universe, and are openly taught in academies and temples across Eukoria. His Chosen Ones are tasked with driving societal progress, overseeing monumental architectural feats, and ensuring that binding contracts are upheld. To aid them in these tasks, Aureon grants his mortal conduits absolute authority over physical substance. The first gift is miraculous transmutation - the ability to instantly reshape base elements to build or repair structures with mathematically perfect precision. The second is the power of absolute dissolution; should an enemy threaten his works or breach a sacred contract, his Chosen can weaponize their logic to mathematically deconstruct the offender and their creations into mere dust.

## [Divine Intercession](https://2e.aonprd.com/Rules.aspx?ID=804)
Aureon rewards those who drive industry forward, utilize pure intellect to manipulate reality, and uphold the sanctity of commerce. Conversely, he punishes those who break contracts, impede progress, or deconstruct the works of artifice without merit.

[Minor Boon::Aureon blesses your craftsmanship and understanding of the physical world. When you get a Critical Fail when Crafting, you get a Fail instead.]
[Moderate Boon::You possess an unnatural understanding of the material world. Once per day, you can cast [Shape Stone](https://2e.aonprd.com/Spells.aspx?ID=1667) or [Mending](https://2e.aonprd.com/Spells.aspx?ID=1597) as an innate arcane spell.]
[Major Boon::Aureon allows your intellect to bypass the standard limits of physical creation. Once, you can Craft any single Common item instantly as a single interact action. You must provide currency equal to the item's value, which is transmuted into the item, and you must be in an area with enough space for the object to manifest, but ignore any other requirements. Once used, this boon is lost.]
  
[Minor Curse::The material world resists your calculations. You constantly suffer a -2 status penalty to Crafting checks, and any item you successfully craft always looks shoddy, despite functioning normally, making it impossible to sell for anywhere near to its true value.]
[Moderate Curse::Aureon decides your wealth is a burden you have not earned. For you, each 50gp worth of coins you carry weigh 1 Bulk. This is consistent regardless of the type of coins the value is stored in, so 5pp, 50gp, 500sp, and 5000cp would each add a bulk. Currency held in gems and other treasure in unaffected, though valuable objects still feel heavier than usual in your hand.]
[Major Curse::The physical substance of your tools rebels against your mind. Whenever you roll a critical failure on a weapon attack roll or a skill check that involves a held tool, that item immediately gains the Broken condition as it spontaneously deconstructs.]
