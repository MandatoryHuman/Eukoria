Solon is the [[1. World Almanac/World/Gods & Divines/Greater Gods/index|Greater God]] of Law and the [[1. World Almanac/World/Gods & Divines/Greater Gods/Canonical Gods/index|Canonical]] patron of Occult Magic. He is formed by the harmonious, cerebral blend of Spirit and Mind. Solon governs the intangible soul and pure consciousness, providing a structured, orthodox path to mastering psychic phenomena, astral projection, and the defence of the mind.

![[Assets/Gods/Solon.webp|400]]

Practitioners of Solon’s tradition, such as bards and psychics, channel his calculated blend of essences to unravel psychic mysteries and construct impenetrable wards against mental intrusion. His most devoted masters are granted the ability to safely untether their consciousness from their physical form to walk the astral planes - or violently sever the astral tethers of their enemies. As a [[1. World Almanac/World/Gods & Divines/Greater Gods/Canonical Gods/index|Canonical]] [[1. World Almanac/World/Gods & Divines/Greater Gods/Canonical Gods/Gods of Magic/index|God of Magic]], his teachings are perfectly widely studied in Eukorian academies.

Despite his pact-bound neutrality regarding the flow of magic, Solon represents absolute, unyielding order. This places him and his followers in direct ideological conflict with [[Lethos, God of Trickery]], whom Solon despises for his mockery of the spirit of the law and his historical murder of [[Thrum, God of Madness]]. Solon's hardliner orthodox followers are notoriously rigid; for instance, they completely despise leaders like [[High Arbiter Garadwen]] of [[1. World Almanac/World/Locations/Eukoria/States/Meridia/index|Meridia]], viewing her loophole-ridden administration as a mockery of justice, even if they are legally unable to prosecute her.

## [Divine Intercession](https://2e.aonprd.com/Rules.aspx?ID=804)

Solon rewards those who bring order to chaos, unravel psychic mysteries, and enforce the absolute truth. He is quick to punish oathbreakers, those who exploit legal loopholes, and those who attempt to subvert the minds of others.

\[Minor Boon::Solon’s absolute order fortifies your mind. You gain a +1 status bonus to saving throws against mental effects and illusions.]
\[Moderate Boon::You project a localized zone of absolute truth. Once per day, you can cast Ring of Truth as an innate Occult spell. Additionally, while within this zone, your attacks deal an additional 1d6 mental damage.]
\[Major Boon::You are granted the power to construct impenetrable wards against mental intrusion. Once per day, you can cast Hidden Mind as an innate Occult spell, though it only targets yourself.]

\[Minor Curse::Bureaucratic red tape binds your very soul. While in a civilised area, any downtime activity you undertake takes twice as long to complete, as you constantly attract the unwarranted suspicion of guards, tax collectors, and officials.]
\[Moderate Curse::Solon's rigid adherence to lawful conduct and his disdain for Lethos's trickery makes it difficult for you to exploit tactical advantages. You ignore the circumstance penalty to AC suffered by off-guard creatures when attacking enemies with the condition.]
\[Major Curse::Because Solon despises trickery and subterfuge, he forces you to fight with honourable, rigid transparency. At the end of each of your turns in combat, you must verbally announce a target. Until the end of your next turn, if you attempt to strike or cast a hostile spell on anyone other than your declared target, you take a -2 status penalty to the attack roll or spell DC.]
